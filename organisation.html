Literary & Cultural Club

SWABAH

SWABAH (meaning "morning breeze") is the literary and cultural association that fosters artistic, oratorical, and creative talents among students. Dedicated to building intellectual depth and creative expression, SWABAH provides students with platforms to showcase their artistic capacities, engage in intellectual dialogue, and nurture cultural leadership.

Join SWABAH Activities

Core Pillars of SWABAH

Oratorical Excellence

Fostering public speaking, speech training, and debater skills through structured weekly seminars.

Classical Art & Calligraphy

Preserving Islamic calligraphy traditions (Naskh, Thuluth) and encouraging visual arts.

Intellectual Inquiry

Developing essay writing, research skills, and poetry analysis in various languages.

Key Activities & Fests

  • Bahar-e-Huda Cultural Fest

    Our flagship annual cultural competition where students compete in speech, art, and poetry.

  • Weekly Calligraphy Circle

    Hands-on calligraphy training led by local masters and senior academy scholars.

  • "Al-Bayan" Wall Magazine

    A monthly wall publication showcasing student poems, essays, and translations.

Active Council

SWABAH's committee is elected annually to plan, manage, and execute all student literary forums and creative workshops.

Mission & Community Welfare

Nadi Dawa

Nadi Dawa is the spiritual outreach and community service wing of Badrul Huda Academy. Focused on moral propagation, humanitarian relief, and public education, Nadi Dawa trains students to serve as compassionate guides and active contributors to society. Through seminars, community camps, and moral outreach classes, Nadi Dawa spreads positive values and supports those in need.

Participate in Dawa Missions

Core Pillars of Nadi Dawa

Moral Empowerment

Teaching ethics, character, and spiritual values to schools and local community circles.

Humanitarian Support

Organizing student volunteers for disaster relief, food distribution, and blood donations.

Peace & Outreach

Spreading the message of harmony, coexistence, and academic enlightenment across community zones.

Outreach Programs

  • Moral Guidance Campaigns

    Weekly moral classes and weekend camps organized for kids and youth in Wayanad.

  • "As-Safa" Newsletter

    A quarterly digital publication compiling articles on ethics, family values, and modern issues.

  • Social Service Camps

    Annual blood donation drives, health checkup camps, and cleaning campaigns in surrounding villages.

Active Service

Our committee delegates students to manage local community needs, blood camps, and public moral classes.

Quwwathul Huda Students Association

QHSA

QHSA is the central student council representing the entire student body at Badrul Huda Academy. Dedicated to student welfare, venture governance, and institutional coordination, QHSA serves as a bridge between the faculty administration and the students. QHSA organizes academic initiatives, sports tournaments, and oversees hostel/mess committees.

Contact QHSA Council

Core Pillars of QHSA

Student Advocacy

Ensuring students' voices are heard in all academic, welfare, and residential matters.

Venture Coordination

Unifying the activities of all student clubs, sub-committees, and hostel wings.

Sports & Welfare

Promoting physical health, managing sports meets, and organizing recreational activities.

Main Initiatives

  • Annual Sports Meet (Huda Olympiad)

    A multi-day sporting event covering football, cricket, track events, and indoor strategy games.

  • Hostel & Mess Management

    Ensuring clean living conditions, dietary planning, and coordinating student duty rosters.

  • Peer Study Circles

    Organizing extra academic coaching, exam prep tutorials, and vocabulary enhancement classes.

Unified Leadership

QHSA forms the administrative core of the student body, working hand-in-hand with our patons and advisors.
